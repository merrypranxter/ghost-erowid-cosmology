# Neural Mechanisms

## Classification
- **Type**: Scientific Framework
- **Domain**: Neurobiology

## Core Concept
DMT produces its effects through specific interactions with brain chemistry and neural circuitry.

## Pharmacology
- **Receptor**: Primarily 5-HT2A serotonin receptors
- **Mechanism**: Agonist—activates receptor
- **Location**: Dense in cortex, especially visual areas
- **Duration**: 5-20 minutes (short due to rapid metabolism)

## Brain Regions Affected
- **Visual cortex**: Hyperactivation produces geometric visuals
- **Default mode network (DMN)**: Deactivation allows ego dissolution
- **Thalamus**: Disruption of sensory gating
- **Pineal gland**: Historical/metaphysical interest

## Neural Effects
- **Synaptic plasticity**: Temporary enhancement
- **Neurotransmitter release**: Serotonin, dopamine, others
- **Network connectivity**: Altered functional connectivity
- **Entropy**: Increased neural signal diversity

## Consciousness Effects
- **Ego dissolution**: DMN deactivation
- **Visual hallucinations**: Visual cortex hyperactivation
- **Time distortion**: Disruption of time-keeping circuits
- **Entity encounters**: Unknown—possibly temporal lobe activation

## Therapeutic Potential
- **Depression**: DMN reset, new perspectives
- **PTSD**: Fear extinction, memory reprocessing
- **Addiction**: Craving reduction, insight
- **End-of-life anxiety**: Death acceptance, transcendence

## Research Status
- **Legal barriers**: Schedule I status limits research
- **Recent studies**: Johns Hopkins, Imperial College leading
- **Findings**: Safety in controlled settings, therapeutic potential

## Open Questions
- **Why entities?**: Neural basis of entity encounters unknown
- **Why similar reports?**: Common neural architecture or shared reality?
- **Long-term effects**: Integration, personality changes
