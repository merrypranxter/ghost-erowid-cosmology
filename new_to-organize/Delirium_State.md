# The Delirium State

## Classification
- **Type**: Altered State of Consciousness
- **Phase**: Peak Deliriant

## Description
Delirium is a state of confused consciousness where reality and hallucination become indistinguishable. Unlike psychedelic states, the operative typically does not know they are hallucinating.

## Characteristics
- **Confusion**: Disoriented, muddled thinking
- **Reality loss**: Cannot distinguish real from unreal
- **Memory impairment**: Poor short-term memory
- **Suggestibility**: Highly influenced by environment
- **Emotional lability**: Rapid mood swings

## Physical Signs
- **Dry mouth**: Severe xerostomia
- **Dilated pupils**: Mydriasis
- **Flushed skin**: Red, warm skin
- **Fever**: Elevated temperature
- **Rapid heartbeat**: Tachycardia
- **Urinary retention**: Difficulty urinating

## Psychological Features
- **Paranoia**: Feeling of being watched, followed
- **Delusions**: False beliefs held with conviction
- **Hallucinations**: True hallucinations, all senses
- **Confabulation**: Making up stories to fill gaps
- **Anxiety**: Intense fear, panic

## Dangers
- **Accidents**: Due to confusion
- **Dehydration**: From dry mouth, fever
- **Overheating**: Hyperthermia risk
- **Psychological**: Trauma from terrifying experiences
- **Physical**: Organ stress, especially heart

## Risk Reduction
- **Sitter**: Essential—someone sober present
- **Dose**: Keep low, especially first time
- **Setting**: Safe, familiar environment
- **Hydration**: Water available, though hard to drink
- **Medical**: Avoid with heart conditions
