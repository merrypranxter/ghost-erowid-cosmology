# The Flange / Robo-Voice

## Classification
- **Type**: Auditory Phenomenon
- **Phase**: All DXM Phases

## Description
The Flange is the characteristic auditory distortion of DXM—voices and sounds take on a robotic, metallic, "flanged" quality.

## Characteristics
- **Robotic**: Voices sound mechanical
- **Metallic**: Tinny, echoing quality
- **Flanged**: Phasing, sweeping effect
- **Delayed**: Echo, reverb
- **Distorted**: Pitch shifts, warping

## Affected Sounds
- **Own voice**: Sounds foreign, robotic
- **Others' voices**: Sound distant, mechanical
- **Music**: Enhanced, transformed
- **Environment**: All sounds affected

## Function
- **Primary**: Dissociation marker
- **Secondary**: Reality distortion signal
- **Tertiary**: Can be aesthetically pleasing

## Musical Enhancement
- **Depth**: Music seems deeper, more complex
- **Separation**: Instruments more distinct
- **Emotion**: Enhanced emotional response
- **Novelty**: Familiar music sounds new

## Technical Notes
- **Effect type**: Similar to audio flange effect
- **Neural basis**: Auditory processing disruption
- **Dose correlation**: More pronounced at higher doses
- **Duration**: Lasts through peak

## Cultural Notes
- **Robo-culture**: DXM community embraces the effect
- **Music**: Certain genres enhanced (electronic, ambient)
- **Communication**: Can make conversation difficult
