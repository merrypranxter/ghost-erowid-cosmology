# Deliriants Dosage Guide

## Classification
- **Type**: Technical Reference
- **Domain**: Practical Information

## WARNING
Deliriants are distinct from psychedelics. They produce true hallucinations (indistinguishable from reality) and delirium. Not recommended for recreational use.

## Datura/Jimson Weed
- **Threshold**: Minimal effects, dry mouth
- **Light**: Strong dry mouth, mild confusion
- **Common**: Significant delirium, hallucinations
- **Strong**: Severe delirium, dangerous
- **Heavy**: Potentially fatal

## DPH (Diphenhydramine)
- **Threshold (100-200 mg)**: Sedation, mild effects
- **Light (200-400 mg)**: Dry mouth, drowsiness
- **Common (400-600 mg)**: Delirium, hallucinations
- **Strong (600-800 mg)**: Severe delirium
- **Heavy (800+ mg)**: Dangerous, potentially fatal

## Scopolamine/Hyoscine
- **Medical dose**: 0.3-0.6 mg (transdermal)
- **Delirium dose**: 0.5-1 mg
- **Toxic dose**: 2+ mg

## Common Effects
- **True hallucinations**: Indistinguishable from reality
- **Delirium**: Confusion, inability to distinguish real/unreal
- **Dry mouth**: Severe xerostomia
- **Dilated pupils**: Photophobia
- **Fever**: Hyperthermia risk
- **Tachycardia**: Rapid heartbeat
- **Urinary retention**: Difficulty urinating
- **Memory loss**: Poor short-term memory

## Dangers
- **Accidents**: Due to confusion
- **Dehydration**: Severe dry mouth
- **Overheating**: Hyperthermia
- **Heart strain**: Tachycardia
- **Psychological**: Traumatic experiences
- **Overdose**: Potentially fatal

## Harm Reduction
- **Sitter**: Essential—someone sober must be present
- **Dose**: Keep as low as possible
- **Setting**: Safe, familiar, clear of hazards
- **Hydration**: Water available
- **Medical**: Avoid with heart conditions
- **Duration**: Effects last 8-24+ hours

## Legal Status
- **DPH**: Legal OTC medication
- **Datura**: Legal in most places
- **Scopolamine**: Prescription/controlled
