# Tracers / Motion Trails

## Classification
- **Type**: Visual Phenomenon
- **Phase**: All LSD Phases

## Description
Tracers are the visual perception of motion trails—objects leave a fading trail behind them as they move, like long-exposure photography.

## Characteristics
- **Appearance**: Fading trail behind moving objects
- **Duration**: Trails linger briefly
- **Color**: Match object or rainbow spectrum
- **Intensity**: Varies with dose and sensitivity
- **Movement**: Any motion creates trails

## Variations
- **Short trails**: Brief, subtle
- **Long trails**: Extended, dramatic
- **Multiple trails**: Several overlapping
- **Rainbow trails**: Color spectrum in trail

## Associated Phenomena
- **Visual snow**: Often co-occurs
- **Pattern enhancement**: Patterns more visible
- **Time perception**: Time may seem slower
- **Movement fascination**: Enjoyment of motion

## Scientific Understanding
- **Neural basis**: Visual persistence, processing delay
- **Dose correlation**: More pronounced at higher doses
- **Individual variation**: Some more susceptible
- **HPPD**: Can persist after experience

## Aesthetic Applications
- **Dance**: Beautiful with moving body
- **Fire spinning**: Dramatic effect
- **Light shows**: Enhanced experience
- **Nature**: Moving water, leaves

## Function
- **Primary**: Visual system effect
- **Secondary**: Aesthetic enhancement
- **Tertiary**: Time perception alteration
