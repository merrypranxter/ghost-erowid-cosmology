# True Hallucinations

## Classification
- **Type**: Perceptual Phenomenon
- **Phase**: Delirium State

## Description
True hallucinations are perceptions that are indistinguishable from reality—unlike the geometric patterns of psychedelics, these appear as solid, real objects, people, or creatures.

## Characteristics
- **Realism**: Indistinguishable from actual perception
- **Solidity**: Appear three-dimensional, physical
- **Persistence**: Don't fade when focused on
- **Integration**: Fit into normal reality seamlessly
- **Conviction**: Operative believes they are real

## Types
- **Visual**: Seeing people, animals, objects
- **Auditory**: Hearing voices, sounds, music
- **Tactile**: Feeling touches, crawling sensations
- **Olfactory**: Smelling scents, odors
- **Gustatory**: Tasting flavors

## Common Manifestations
- **Insects**: Spiders, ants, bugs
- **People**: Known or unknown individuals
- **Animals**: Pets, creatures
- **Objects**: Items that don't exist
- **Scenarios**: Complex situations, events

## Distinguishing from Reality
- **Inconsistencies**: Small details wrong
- **Impossibility**: Violates physical laws
- **Sitter input**: Others can't see them
- **Reality testing**: Check physical evidence

## Function
- **Primary**: Delirium state indicator
- **Secondary**: Reality testing challenge
- **Tertiary**: Psychological material manifestation
