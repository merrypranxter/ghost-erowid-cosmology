# Dissociation / The Separation

## Classification
- **Type**: Psychological Phenomenon
- **Phase**: All DXM Phases

## Description
Dissociation is the core DXM experience—a separation of consciousness from the body, from the mind, from normal identity, creating a space of detached observation.

## Types
- **Depersonalization**: Self feels unreal, distant
- **Derealization**: World feels unreal, distant
- **Out-of-body**: Consciousness leaves body
- **Ego dissolution**: Self-concept dissolves

## Stages
1. **Mild**: Slight distance from body/mind
2. **Moderate**: Clear separation, floating sensation
3. **Strong**: Out-of-body, observing from outside
4. **Complete**: No self, no body, pure consciousness

## Associated Phenomena
- **The Flange**: Auditory distortion, robotic sound
- **Robo-walk**: Uncoordinated movement
- **Nausea**: Common on come-up
- **Itch**: Histamine release

## Positive Aspects
- **Detachment**: Freedom from emotional reactivity
- **Insight**: Seeing patterns clearly
- **Peace**: Freedom from identification
- **Play**: Reality as game

## Challenging Aspects
- **Disorientation**: Loss of grounding
- **Anxiety**: Fear of losing control
- **Nausea**: Physical discomfort
- **Confusion**: Difficulty thinking clearly

## Integration
- **Insight**: Lessons from detachment
- **Perspective**: New view on problems
- **Acceptance**: Reduced reactivity
- **Playfulness**: Lighter approach to life
